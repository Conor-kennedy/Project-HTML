Club History

The club was founded in 1969 from the merger of Ballyboden Wanderers and Rathfarnham St. Endas. The clubhouse is located on the Firhouse Road. With over 100 teams from juvenile up to adults playing hurling, mens football, ladies football and camogie, it is one of the largest GAA clubs in the country. In recent years, the club has been successful winning county championships at adult level in all four codes including one All Ireland Football Championship in 2016. There are a number of facilities to cater for the club members. Other events that are carried out throughout the year include snooker tournaments, golf outings, drama and music.
